A Curated Odyssey

Egyptian Heritage Halls

A curated journey through millennia of Egyptian civilization, spanning from the Pharaonic glory to the Coptic and Islamic eras. It offers visitors a rare glimpse into the artifacts, traditions, and architectural wonders that define Egypt's timeless legacy.

The Great Institutions

Custodians of global human history

Grand Egyptian Museum

Giza Plateau Details

The world's largest archaeological museum dedicated to a single civilization. Located on the Giza plateau, it houses over 100,000 artifacts including the complete Tutankhamun collection.

The Coptic Museum

Coptic Cairo, Old Cairo Details

Founded in 1908 in Old Cairo, it houses the largest collection of Coptic Christian artifacts in the world, tracing the era's unique fusion of cultures.

Egyptian Museum

Tahrir Square, Cairo Details

Established in 1902, this landmark in Tahrir Square remains the central archive of Pharaonic history, housing the iconic Tanis treasure.

Islamic Art Museum

Bab Al-Khalq, Cairo Details

A global masterpiece housing one of the most comprehensive collections of Islamic artifacts, spanning wood carving, textiles, and ceramics.

NMEC

Ain Al-Sira, Cairo Details

The National Museum of Egyptian Civilization is a modern architectural marvel in Fustat, famously housing the Royal Mummies.
